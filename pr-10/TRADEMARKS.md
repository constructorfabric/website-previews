## 1. Purpose

Constructor Fabric Foundation is a nonprofit organization supporting open-source
technology and its community.

This policy helps users identify official Constructor Fabric Foundation projects,
services, communications, and activities while allowing broad and truthful
discussion and use of Constructor Fabric open-source technologies.

The Foundation's software and website source may be available under open-source
licenses. Those licenses do not authorize anyone to represent themselves as the
Foundation or to imply that an independent product, service, event, website,
organization, account, or communication is official, sponsored, approved, or
endorsed by the Foundation.

## 3. Uses That Do Not Normally Require Permission

You may use Constructor Fabric word marks truthfully to:

- refer to Constructor Fabric Foundation or its projects;
- state that software uses, supports, or is compatible with Constructor Fabric software;
- identify software as being based on or forked from Constructor Fabric software;
- link to an official Constructor Fabric Foundation website or repository;
- discuss, review, criticize, teach, report on, or compare Constructor Fabric technologies;
- reproduce attribution or trademark notices required by an applicable license.

Such use must be accurate and must not imply sponsorship, endorsement,
certification, partnership, affiliation, authorization, or official status.

## 4. Uses Requiring Prior Written Permission

Prior written permission is required before:

- using a Constructor Fabric logo except as technically necessary to reproduce an
  unmodified copy of the official website for archival or development purposes;
- using a Constructor Fabric Mark as part of a company, organization, domain,
  product, service, application, event, conference, publication, social-media
  account, or community name;
- using a Constructor Fabric Mark prominently in advertising or merchandise;
- claiming that a product, service, release, event, organization, or communication
  is official, approved, certified, sponsored, supported, or endorsed by the Foundation;
- representing yourself as an employee, agent, partner, chapter, spokesperson,
  representative, or other authorized actor of the Foundation.

## 5. Prohibited Uses

You may not:

- impersonate Constructor Fabric Foundation;
- falsely claim to act for, bind, or speak on behalf of the Foundation;
- use Constructor Fabric Marks in a deceptive, misleading, defamatory, or unlawful manner;
- create a likelihood of confusion regarding source, sponsorship, affiliation,
  endorsement, approval, or official status;
- register or use confusingly similar domains, organization names, company names,
  product names, service names, account names, or project names;
- alter or combine a Constructor Fabric logo with another logo without written permission;
- use Constructor Fabric Marks in connection with fraudulent or deceptive activity.

## 6. Forks and Modified Versions

Open-source licenses may permit copying and modification of the website and other
Constructor Fabric software. A modified or independently operated version must
not be presented as an official Constructor Fabric Foundation website, service,
release, organization, or communication.

Unless expressly authorized, operators and distributors of forks must:

- remove Constructor Fabric Foundation logos from public-facing presentations;
- use a distinct name and visual identity;
- avoid names, domains, layouts, or presentation likely to imply official status;
- clearly state that the fork is independent and is not affiliated with, sponsored
  by, endorsed by, or operated by Constructor Fabric Foundation.

Truthful statements describing the software's origin remain permitted.

Contributing code, documentation, funding, services, or other assistance does not
make a person or organization an employee, agent, partner, spokesperson, or
authorized representative of Constructor Fabric Foundation.

Only persons expressly authorized under the Foundation's governance rules or in
writing by an authorized officer may act, bind, or speak on behalf of the Foundation.
